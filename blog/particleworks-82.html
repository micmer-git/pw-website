Particleworks v8.2 New Release

Better post-processing, advanced air solvers and a new explicit method for pressure.

March 2026 6 min read Product Update

CFD software updates usually bring a mix of minor UI tweaks and one big feature you might never actually use in production. Particleworks v8.2 is different. It's a heavy-hitting release focused strictly on what engineers have been asking for: better multiphase coupling, stable pressure solvers, and tools to make massive simulations physically manageable.

If you are running complex fluid-structure interactions, splashing gearboxes, or e-motor cooling scenarios, here is a no-nonsense look at the new capabilities under the hood, pulled directly from the v8.2 feature log.

Fluids: EIMPS Method Kills Pressure Oscillations

Let's talk about the MPS (Moving Particle Semi-implicit) solver. Explicit solvers are brilliant for sheer calculation speed, but they often suffer from annoying pressure oscillations and require you to artificially tweak the speed of sound just to keep the simulation stable.

Version 8.2 introduces the EIMPS (Explicit-Incompressible MPS) method.

  • It inherently suppresses pressure oscillations during calculations, providing much cleaner data outputs.
  • Unlike older pressure explicit solvers, you no longer need to set the speed of sound.

This translates to more reliable data when analyzing high-speed rotors or pumps. Additionally, you can now calculate Heat Transfer Coefficients (HTC) via the Nusselt number directly on insulated (adiabatic) walls, giving you more flexibility in thermal setups without needing complex workarounds.

The Air Game: Massive FVM & LBM Enhancements

Aerodynamics and multiphase flows received a massive, practical upgrade in this release.

FVM (Finite Volume Method)

  • Single-precision execution to speed up iterative solver convergence (available when AMG is disabled).
  • Partial Lattice Generation — saves RAM by generating FVM cells only where necessary via shape file.
  • FVM now supports direct heat transfer coupling with CHT (Conjugate Heat Transfer) cells.
  • Specific drag adjustment parameters for different material properties in FVM-MPS coupling.

LBM (Lattice Boltzmann Method)

  • True two-way coupling between LBM and MPS via the Immersed Boundary Method (IBM). Instead of treating particles as isolated aerosols, the solver considers interaction between non-isolated liquid bodies and airflow. Simulate bulk liquids splitting apart and deforming due to air shear.
  • New Cumulant K17 collision model for high-precision, low-numerical-viscosity aerodynamics.

Thermal & Powders: Anisotropy and Deposition

Heat conduction isn't always isotropic. CHT internal heat conduction simulations in 8.2 now support anisotropic thermal conductivity.

  • Set thermal conductivity individually for each spatial direction.
  • This is exactly what you need when modeling the laminated structures of motor stator cores, where heat moves differently radially versus axially.

DEM (Discrete Element Method)

  • New Deposition model — simulate powders and snow adhering to walls under air resistance.
  • Coupled with LBM, predict flow channel blockages from material buildup.
  • New mass/volumetric flowrate boundary condition for DEM.

Post-Processing That Doesn't Eat Your Time (Or Storage)

We spend half our lives in post-processing. v8.2 brings serious quality-of-life improvements:

  • Streamlines: Generate visual streamlines directly for FVM mesh and LBM airflow fields.
  • Force Mapping: Map normal and tangential fluid resistance forces onto polygon structures.
  • CSV Probes: Import probe positions via CSV file instead of clicking them manually.
  • Net Flowrate: Flow-rate measurements now output net flow (forward minus backward), with customizable output intervals.
  • File Size Control: LBM and DEM allow specific output regions or skip writing heavy restart data. Copy and paste multiple nodes across project scenes.
