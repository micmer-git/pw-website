Overview

What is Granuleworks

Granuleworks is an advanced DEM (Discrete Element Method) solver for simulating the behaviour of bulk solids and powders. It is installed together with Particleworks and shares the same graphical interface, making it straightforward to set up coupled fluid-powder simulations. Model granular flows, predict material segregation, analyse powder transport, and simulate filling and emptying processes — all with GPU-accelerated performance.

Capabilities

What you can do with Granuleworks

Discrete Element Method

Newton's 2nd Law applied to individual particles. Simulate bulk solids and powders with realistic contact mechanics, friction and energy dissipation.

Material properties

User-defined granulometry, bulk density, angle of repose, adhesion, friction coefficients. Calibrate to match real-world material behaviour.

Multi-phase coupling

MPS-DEM for fluid-powder mixing, FVM-DEM and LBM-DEM for air-driven powder transport and fluidisation simulations.

Flexible body coupling

RecurDyn integration for deformable structures interacting with granular materials. Simulate conveyor belts, flexible walls and rubber liners.

Thermal simulations

Temperature evolution in powders with particle-to-particle and particle-to-wall conduction models. Track heat fronts through granular beds.

GPU acceleration

Fast simulations with multi-GPU support. Handle millions of particles in practical turnaround times for industrial-scale problems.

Scalable DEM (SDEM)

Coarse-graining technique that represents clusters of fine particles as larger computational particles, enabling simulation of very fine powders.

Non-spherical particles

Capture anisotropic behaviour of real-world granules. Model elongated, flat or irregular particle shapes for more accurate flow predictions.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

The Discrete Element Method (DEM) is a numerical technique for simulating the motion of large numbers of individual particles. Each particle is tracked individually: forces (gravity, contact, friction, cohesion) are computed from Newton's second law, and particle positions and velocities are updated at each time step. DEM is the standard approach for modelling granular materials such as sand, powders, pellets, rocks and agricultural grains, where the bulk behaviour emerges from the interactions between millions of individual particles.

Granuleworks is the DEM solver module developed by Prometech Software and distributed alongside Particleworks. It is installed as part of the same software package and shares the identical graphical user interface, project structure and post-processing tools. This tight integration means engineers can set up coupled simulations — for example, liquid and powder mixing (MPS-DEM) or air-driven powder transport (LBM-DEM) — within a single project file, without any data translation or file exchange between separate tools.

Granular flow simulation with DEM is used in a wide range of industries:

Mining and minerals: Ore crushing, screening, conveyor transfer points, stockpile formation.

Pharmaceuticals: Powder blending, tablet coating, capsule filling, fluidised-bed drying.

Food processing: Grain handling, cereal mixing, sugar and flour transport.

Construction materials: Concrete mixing, aggregate flow, silo discharge.

Automotive and industrial: Snow accumulation and clearing simulation, powder coating, additive manufacturing powder spreading.

Scalable DEM (SDEM) is a coarse-graining technique available in Granuleworks. In standard DEM, every physical particle is represented individually, which can become prohibitively expensive when dealing with very fine powders (particle diameters below ~100 microns) that result in billions of particles. SDEM groups clusters of small particles into larger representative particles, scaling contact forces and material properties accordingly. This makes it practical to simulate fine powder processes — such as pharmaceutical blending or additive manufacturing powder beds — at industrial scale while retaining the essential bulk-level physics.
