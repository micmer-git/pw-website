Overview

What is Particleworks

Particleworks is a cutting-edge CAE software based on the innovative mesh-less Moving Particle Simulation (MPS) method. It solves the Navier-Stokes equations without requiring a computational mesh, enabling engineers to go from CAD geometry to running simulation in a single day. Free-surface flows, splashing, sloshing, multi-phase mixing — problems that are notoriously painful with traditional CFD become straightforward with a particle-based approach.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Particle simulation software models the behaviour of fluids, granular materials or other continua by representing them as a large collection of discrete particles rather than solving equations on a fixed computational mesh. Each particle carries physical properties — mass, velocity, temperature — and interacts with its neighbours according to the governing equations of motion. This approach is particularly powerful for problems involving free surfaces, splashing, fragmentation and large deformations, where traditional mesh-based methods struggle to maintain accuracy.

Particleworks is a commercial CAE software developed by Prometech Software in Japan and distributed in Europe by Particleworks Europe. It uses the Moving Particle Simulation (MPS) method to solve incompressible Navier-Stokes equations without a computational mesh. Engineers import CAD geometry, define fluid regions and boundary conditions, and run the solver — typically going from raw geometry to first results within a single working day. The software is widely adopted in automotive, industrial machinery, food processing and energy sectors for oil flow, cooling, sloshing and mixing analyses.

Moving Particle Simulation (MPS) is a meshfree computational method originally developed by Professor Seiichi Koshizuka at the University of Tokyo. It discretises the fluid domain into particles and approximates differential operators — gradient, divergence, Laplacian — using weighted averages over neighbouring particles. The method naturally tracks free surfaces, handles topology changes such as merging and splitting, and avoids the mesh distortion issues that plague Lagrangian finite-element or ALE approaches. MPS solves the incompressible Navier-Stokes equations through a semi-implicit pressure correction scheme, ensuring mass conservation and numerical stability even in violent flow scenarios.

In mesh-based CFD (e.g. finite volume, finite element), the simulation domain is divided into a structured or unstructured grid of cells. The governing equations are solved at each cell, and accuracy depends heavily on mesh quality. Generating a high-quality mesh for complex geometries can take days or weeks and often requires significant manual effort.

In mesh-less CFD — as implemented in Particleworks — there is no mesh. The fluid is represented by moving particles, and the equations of motion are evaluated through particle-to-particle interactions. This eliminates meshing entirely, dramatically reduces setup time, and naturally handles free surfaces, large deformations and multi-phase interfaces. The trade-off is that meshfree methods can be more computationally intensive per degree of freedom, but modern GPU acceleration (as used in Particleworks) largely compensates for this.

Particle simulation is used across many industries. Common applications include:

Automotive: Oil splashing and churning inside gearboxes and transmissions, oil jet cooling of gears and bearings, sloshing in fuel tanks, water management on vehicle exteriors.

Electric vehicles: Direct oil cooling of e-motor stators and rotors, inverter cooling, thermal management of battery packs.

Industrial machinery: Hydraulic systems, lubrication circuits, pump and valve flow analysis.

Food and chemicals: Mixing of viscous or non-Newtonian fluids such as chocolate, dough and adhesives. Filling and pouring simulations.

Civil and environmental: Dam-break analysis, flood propagation, coastal wave impact on structures.

Particleworks offers a comprehensive feature set for meshfree CFD simulation:

Fluid dynamics: Incompressible Navier-Stokes via MPS, explicit and semi-implicit pressure solvers (including the new EIMPS method), free-surface tracking, surface tension, wettability models.

Multi-phase: Multiple immiscible fluids with different densities and viscosities, oil-water-air interactions.

Thermal: Conjugate heat transfer, HTC mapping, anisotropic solid conductivity, Nusselt-based wall HTC.

Air solvers: Finite Volume Method (FVM) for steady-state or transient airflow, Lattice-Boltzmann Method (LBM) for aerodynamics and two-way air-liquid coupling.

Rheology: Non-Newtonian viscosity models (power law, Bingham, Cross, Herschel-Bulkley) and user-defined viscosity functions.

Performance: Multi-GPU acceleration with near-linear scaling, multi-resolution particle refinement, automatic domain decomposition.

Post-processing: Streamlines, force mapping, probe-based monitoring, CSV export, flow-rate measurement, VTK output.

Particleworks supports direct coupling with several leading engineering tools:

ANSYS Workbench: Bi-directional coupling for thermal and structural analysis. Export HTC maps and pressure loads to ANSYS Mechanical or Fluent.

Romax (Hexagon): Integration for transmission and gearbox simulations, combining Particleworks oil-flow results with Romax bearing and gear analysis.

RecurDyn (FunctionBay): Multi-body dynamics coupling for fluid-structure interaction. Simulate moving components interacting with liquids or powders in real time.

modeFRONTIER (ESTECO): Design optimisation platform that can drive Particleworks simulations parametrically, exploring the design space to find optimal geometries and operating conditions.

Additionally, Particleworks exports standard formats (CSV, VTK, STL) for integration with virtually any post-processing or analysis toolchain.
